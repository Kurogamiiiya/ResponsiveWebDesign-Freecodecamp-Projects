Dr. Ir. H. Soekarno

1st President of Indonesia

Soekarno is one of the most important figures in Indonesian history. Soekarno was born on 6 June 1901 in Surabaya, East Java, and died on 21 June 1970 in Jakarta. Soekarno had an important role in Indonesian independence, namely as the proclaimer of Indonesian independence and Indonesia's first president.

Soekarno biography

Family and Education

  • Soekarno was born to Raden Soekemi Sosrodihardjo and Ida Ayu Nyoman Rai on June 6 1901. His father was a teacher at a Javanese elementary school and his mother was the daughter of a Balinese noble family. Soekarno was the third of four children. As a child, Soekarno lived with his grandfather, Raden Hardjokromo in Tulungagung, East Java.
  • Next, Soekarno entered elementary school at Eerste Inlandse School in Mojokerto and in 1911, Soekarno moved to Europeesche Lagere School. After completing basic education, Soekarno continued his education at HBS (Hogere Burger School) in Surabaya.
  • Soekarno lived with H.O.S Cokroaminoto, the leader of the Syarekat Islam (SI) movement organization. He then continued his education majoring in civil engineering at THS (Technische Hoogeschool) Bandung in 1921 and obtained a civil engineer (Ir.) degree in 1927.

Political Life

  • Soekarno became active in the world of politics since living in Surabaya. His thinking was influenced by H.O.S Cokroaminoto and his comrades in arms such as Alimin, Musso, Darsono, Haji Agus Salim and Abdul Muis. The young Soekarno was also active in the Tri Koro Dharmo organization and wrote articles in the daily Oetoesan Indies.
  • During his education at THS, the Algemene Study Club was founded in 1926, which later became the forerunner of PNI. In 1929, because of his political activities, Soekarno was arrested and exiled to Ende, Flores, because he was considered involved in the nationalist movement which was considered to be destroying colonial order. During his exile, Soekarno wrote many essays and speeches that inspired the Indonesian independence movement.
  • In 1930, Sukarno was transferred to Sukamiskin prison. Soekarno was freed from prison after reading his plea entitled Indonesia Sues before the Dutch East Indies government, PNI members and the general public.

Soekarno's Struggle during the Japanese Colonization Period

  • Sukarno was released from detention in 1942. At first, Sukarno supported the arrival of Japan. The policy of establishing organizations such as Jawa Hokokai, Center for People's Power (Putera), BPUPKI and PPKI became Japan's influence in inviting nationalists to join.

the proclamation of independence

  • At the time of independence, Sukarno and Mohammad Hatta proclaimed independence as a form of Indonesia's release from the shackles of colonialism. He was appointed as Indonesia's first president and led Indonesia until 1967. During his reign, Soekarno fought for Indonesia's independence and sovereignty.

President Soekarno's term of office

  • During his tenure as president, Soekarno had many achievements in the international world. Some of the successes achieved were the success of the Asia-Africa Conference (KAA), the formation of the Non-Aligned Movement (NAM) and diplomatic relations with large countries such as the Soviet Union and China.
  • In the period from 1963 to 1966, Sukarno sparked tensions with Malaysia by arguing that he did not agree to the merger of the Malayan Federation which consisted of Malaysia, Singapore and the British Empire's colonies.

End of Life

  • In 1965 the G30S incident occurred which was the beginning of the decline of Sukarno's leadership. As a result of the G30S, Indonesia's condition was unstable, which required Soekarno to act by issuing the Sebelas Maret (Supersemar) order. It was from this letter that the leadership mandate moved to Suharto until officially in 1967 President Sukarno was replaced by Suharto. He died on June 21 1970 in Jakarta and was buried in Blitar, East Java.
